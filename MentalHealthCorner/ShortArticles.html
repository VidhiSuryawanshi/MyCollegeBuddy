🧠 Questions to Ask Yourself

Take a moment. These questions are here to help you reconnect with your thoughts, feelings, and goals.

  • When did I last truly enjoy learning, without worrying about marks?
  • Am I setting boundaries between study time and personal time?
  • What does success really mean to me — beyond grades?
  • Is my stress coming from fear of failure or the pressure to be perfect?
  • How do I talk to myself when I make mistakes — kindly or critically?
  • Do I ask for help when I need it, or do I try to manage everything alone?
  • Am I driven more by growth, comparison, or expectations?
  • Do I celebrate small wins, or only big achievements?
  • Am I taking on too much just to prove myself?
  • When was the last time I said “I’m proud of myself” and truly meant it?

🌟 Real-Life Comebackss

These real-life comebacks remind us that failure is just a stepping stone to success.

  • J.K. Rowling: Battled depression and faced rejection before Harry Potter changed the world.
  • Steve Jobs: Fired from Apple, came back stronger and changed tech forever.
  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: Rejected as a pilot, became the “Missile Man of India.”
  • Oprah Winfrey: Told she wasn’t fit for TV — became a global icon.
  • Thomas Edison: Failed 1,000 times before creating the light bulb.
  • Colonel Sanders: Rejected 1009 times before KFC became a hit.
  • Stephen King: His first novel “Carrie” was rejected 30 times. Now he’s a legend.
  • Michael Jordan: Cut from his school team, but became the GOAT.
  • Neeraj Chopra: Overcame body image issues to win India’s first Olympic gold in athletics.
